# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

AutoOutlook is an open-source severe-weather dashboard and artifact pipeline. Project participants are expected to help keep this community respectful, practical, and welcoming for contributors with different levels of experience.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that supports a healthy project include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of different viewpoints and experience levels.
- Giving constructive feedback that focuses on the work, not the person.
- Accepting responsibility for mistakes and correcting them when possible.
- Prioritizing the safety and reliability goals of the project.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or sustained disruption of discussions.
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or derogatory comments.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Any conduct that a maintainer reasonably determines is inappropriate in a professional open-source setting.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, commits, community chat, and any public or private communication related to AutoOutlook.

## Reporting

Report unacceptable behavior to the maintainer at `info@autooutlook.tech`. Please include relevant links, screenshots, logs, and a short description of what happened. Do not publish sensitive reports in a public issue.

The maintainer will review reports promptly and may remove comments, close threads, restrict participation, or take other action needed to protect the project community.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate, including:

1. A private or public correction.
2. A formal warning.
3. Temporary restrictions from project spaces.
4. Permanent removal from project spaces.

Enforcement decisions should be based on the impact of the behavior, the available evidence, and the need to keep the project safe and productive.
